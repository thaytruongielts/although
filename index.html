Slide 1: Introduction to 'Although' & 'In Spite of'

'Although' and 'In Spite of' are used to show a **contrast** or a surprising result.

They have a similar meaning but are used with different **grammatical structures**.

Slide 2: Using 'Although,' 'Though,' & 'Even Though'

These words are **conjunctions** and are followed by a **subject + verb**.

  • '**Although**': I didn't get the job **although I had** the necessary qualifications.
  • '**Though**': You'll find me at home **though I'm** usually out on Saturdays. (often used more casually or at the end of a sentence)
  • '**Even Though**': is a stronger form of 'although' and emphasizes the contrast.
    _Even though it was raining, we enjoyed our holiday._

Slide 3: Using 'In Spite of' & 'Despite'

These are **prepositions** and are followed by a **noun, pronoun, or -ing form of a verb**.

  • '**In Spite of**': _In spite of **the rain**, we enjoyed our holiday._
  • '**Despite**': _Despite **the traffic**, we arrived on time._
The phrase '**despite the fact that**' is often used before a **subject and verb**, similar to 'although.'
_Despite the fact that she was tired, she kept working._
